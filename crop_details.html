Major Crops

Rice

A rice crop involves cultivating semi-aquatic rice plants, typically on flat, water-retentive soil and requires consistent irrigation, with careful field preparation, planting, and weed management.

Nutritions Values:

Rice is an excellent source of carbohydrates, which are the body’s main energy source. It contains a modest amount of protein, which is essential for building and repairing tissues. Brown rice is a good source of dietary fiber, which promotes digestive health.

More
Potato

Potato Potatoes are underground stem tubers of the plant Solanum tuberosum, a perennial in the nightshade family Solanaceae. Potato cultivars appear in a variety of colors, shapes, and sizes.

Nutritions Values:

Potatoes are primarily a source of complex carbohydrates, which are essential for energy for the body and brain. The skin of the potato contains a good amount of fiber, which aids in digestion and can help you feel full longer.

More
Tomato

Tomato The tomato is a plant whose fruit is an edible berry that is eaten as a vegetable. The tomato is a member of the nightshade family that includes tobacco, potato, and chili peppers.

Nutritions Values:

Tomatoes are an excellent source of Vitamin C and also provide significant amounts of Vitamin K and folate (Vitamin B9). They are a good source of potassium, which is important for heart health and blood pressure control.
