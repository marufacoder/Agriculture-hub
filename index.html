About Our Agricultural Hub

A Hub is a center of effective business activities which operates as a one-stop shop where various products and services are offered. The concept aims at addressing challenges faced by small scale farmers in accessing advice on farming, affordable inputs, markets and other services required for profitable agri-business ventures. Hub creates an ecosystem where collaborations and knowledge transfer can occur in order to spur innovation and business opportunities, thus benefitting the greater network. Agriculture Sector Transformation and Growth Strategy (ASTGS) 2019-2029 envisages that the hub trade should enable co-existence among the private sector players, county governments and other stakeholders in the Agricultural sector. In this regard, NCPB has positioned itself to partner with private sector players in its commercial programs and activities through its commercial arm; The Hub holds various agricultural products including but not limited to food items, farm inputs, farm implements and agrochemicals.

Agricultural Hub Purpose

Market Access and Value Chain Enhancement: Collecting high-quality produce from smallholders to meet the volume requirements of large-scale buyers and processors.

Access to Inputs and Technology: Providing easier, localized access to high-quality seedlings, fertilizers, seeds, and agrochemicals.

Knowledge Exchange and Training: Delivering training on modern, climate-smart agricultural techniques, crop husbandry, and Good Agricultural Practices (GAP).

Economic and Social Development: Creating employment and business opportunities for young people as hub operators/entrepreneurs.

Crop Details

Rice

Rice is a semi-aquatic, staple cereal crop cultivated globally, predominantly in Asia, which produces 594.5 million tons annually. It requires consistent irrigation, thriving in clay/silt soils. Cultivation involves direct sowing or transplanting seedlings, with high-yield management requiring proper nutrient application and pest control.

Potato

The potato is a starchy, tuberous crop from the perennial nightshade family, ranking as the world's third most important food crop. Originating in the Andes, potatoes are highly nutritious, providing significant vitamin C, potassium, and carbohydrates. They are grown globally in over 100 countries and are a staple food for millions.

Tomato

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is a warm-season, mainly annual crop requiring full sun, well-drained, nutrient-rich soil (pH 5.5–7.0), and 18°C–27°C temperatures. Cultivation involves staking, regular irrigation, and high nitrogen/potassium inputs, with a 60–100+ day maturity period.

Pest Control

Natural Predators

Natural predators, or beneficial insects, are critical for sustainable crop protection, reducing pest populations by up to 103% in fruit trees and 51% in cereal crops. Key predators include ladybugs (aphids), lacewings, parasitic wasps, hoverflies, spiders, and ground beetles. .

Resistant varities

Resistant crop varieties are genetically bred or modified plants designed to withstand pests, diseases (e.g., fungi, bacteria, viruses), and environmental stresses like drought or salinity. By strengthening plants, these varieties reduce reliance on chemical pesticides.

Targeted Pesticides

Targeted pesticides are specialized chemicals or biological agents designed to control specific pests—such as insects, weeds, or fungi—while minimizing harm to non-target organisms and the environment. Unlike broad-spectrum options, these include selective herbicides, fungicides.
